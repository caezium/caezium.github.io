**Market Equilibrium**  
exists when the [[Demand]] for a product matches the [[Supply]] so there is no excess [[Demand]].

price-quantity graphs - an x shape always, with price on the y-axis  
The [[Demand]] curve shows that consumers will tend to buy more as the price falls.  
The [[Supply]] curve shows that firms tend to offer more for sale as the prices increase.

- Equilibrium in a market occurs when **[[Demand]] = [[Supply]]**
- At this point the price is called the **market clearing price**
    - This is the **price** at which sellers are clearing (selling) their stock **at an acceptable rate**

## equilibrium price
also referred to as the *market clearing price*
**where [[Demand]] and [[Supply]] are equal at that price**
**there are no shortages or surpluses of the product**
