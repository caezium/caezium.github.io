The quantity and quality of the [[Factors of Production]] will change if there is a change in the demand for the supply.
	changes in the costs of [[Factors of Production]]
		ex. higher [[Labor]] costs caused by an increase in the national minimum wage.
	innovation
		ex. better education means higher quality of [[Labor]], better technologically advanced [[Capital]] means better quality and can reduce the quantity needed.
Government policies can also affect the costs of production.
ex. the use of taxes and subsidies. lower income taxes can help create incentives to work, increasing the quantity of [[Labor]] resources. subsidies reduce the production costs, increasing output of [[Goods]] and services.

Innovation and new technologies allow firms to produce more output. higher productivity also enables firms to cut their costs.

Bad weather conditions (ex. droughts or floods) reduce the supply of agricultural products. good weather might also increase supply.
